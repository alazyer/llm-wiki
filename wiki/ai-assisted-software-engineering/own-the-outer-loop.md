# Own the Outer Loop

## Summary
Agentic engineering shifts humans out of the inner execution loop and into the outer accountability loop. Agents can investigate, implement, verify, and repeat, but humans must own quality signals, verdicts, answerability, constraints, sampling, audits, and production consequences.

## Key Takeaways
- Quality is the evidence-producing system of tests, checks, logs, hooks, sandboxes, and monitors.
- Verdict is the production decision: ship, block, redirect, narrow, add a guardrail, or reject.
- Answerability means someone can explain what changed, why it was safe enough, and what happens if it is wrong.
- Agents provide capability inside the system; humans retain agency at the boundary.
- AI-generated code widens the trust-verification gap because creation gets cheaper faster than review and control.
- Hidden costs include cognitive surrender, cognitive debt, and orchestration tax.
- Back pressure is how autonomy stays safe: scopes, worktrees, evidence, audit logs, opt-in permissions, and clear ownership.
- The bottleneck moves from “can we build this?” to “should this exist, can we answer for it?”
