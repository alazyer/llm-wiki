# How to Become AI-Native

## Summary
Being AI-native is an organizational design choice, not simply using chatbots or copilots. The company must make its workflows, data, policies, permissions, and success criteria explicit enough that agents can do the structured first pass and humans can supervise ambiguity, trust, taste, and exceptions.

## Key Takeaways
- AI-assisted companies add AI at the edges; AI-native companies redesign core workflows assuming agents perform the first structured 80%.
- Agents cannot operate on scattered truth, undocumented exceptions, hidden institutional memory, or process debt living in people’s heads.
- The work is operationally boring but strategic: clean data, document SOPs, define policies, clarify source-of-truth systems, and create audit trails.
- Humans become reviewers and designers of judgment boundaries rather than search engines, routers, copywriters, and data movers.
- Start with one narrow, high-volume, economically meaningful workflow such as support resolution, outbound prospecting, onboarding, claims intake, renewals, or reporting.
- Map triggers, required data, decisions, reversible actions, approval points, success metrics, and error modes before adding agents.
- Put agents in the workflow with boundaries: draft, classify, enrich, summarize, recommend, and act only where risk is understood.
- Judge AI-native progress by business metrics: resolution time, conversion rate, gross margin, revenue per employee, error rate, customer satisfaction, sales velocity, and renewal rate.
