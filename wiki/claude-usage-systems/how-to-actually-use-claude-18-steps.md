# How to Actually Use Claude: 18 Steps

## Summary
Effective Claude use starts with environment and context, not clever one-off prompts. Projects, custom instructions, style examples, and clean chat boundaries make Claude a thinking partner rather than a generic answer box.

## Key Takeaways
- Use Projects for persistent context instead of scattering work across standalone chats.
- Define who you are, what you are working on, preferred style, and boundaries.
- Ask Claude to ask questions before solving ambiguous tasks.
- Use Claude for thinking, critique, planning, rewriting, and stress-testing, not only answers.
- Save tokens with explicit length, no-preamble preferences, and fresh chats for new topics.
- Style cloning works best with real examples and explicit audience/context.
- Strong prompts turn Claude into tutor, analyst, travel planner, finance explainer, or business sparring partner.
