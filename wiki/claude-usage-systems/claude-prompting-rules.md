# Claude Prompting Rules

## Summary
Prompting works best when Claude receives role, audience, context, constraints, desired format, examples, and iteration instructions. The main upgrade is moving from vague requests to structured collaboration.

## Key Takeaways
- Specify role and audience so Claude can choose the right language and level.
- Paste full context and say what the context is for.
- Negative constraints matter: say what not to do.
- Ask for exact length, structure, style, examples, and alternatives.
- Chain tasks instead of combining research, analysis, writing, and editing in one prompt.
- Use fresh chats to avoid context bleed.
- Ask Claude to critique, self-rate, and tell you what you missed.
- Constraints often increase creativity by narrowing the search space.
