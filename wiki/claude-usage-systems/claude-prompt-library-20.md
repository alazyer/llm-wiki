# 20 Claude Prompts Library

## Summary
The prompt library turns Claude into repeatable roles: personal assistant, editor, coach, analyst, tutor, negotiator, planner, and devil’s advocate. The reusable pattern is structured context plus a specific role and output format.

## Key Takeaways
- Research prompts should synthesize, compare, challenge assumptions, and extract mental models.
- Writing prompts can mimic style, rewrite for audiences, expand bullets, and act as a brutal editor.
- Career prompts can prepare negotiations, interviews, resumes, and difficult conversations.
- Life prompts can plan meals, travel, finances, legal/plain-English reviews, and personal systems.
- Learning prompts should use Feynman explanations, curricula, quizzes, and Socratic tutoring.
- The value is not the exact wording; it is the reusable structure and explicit success criteria.
