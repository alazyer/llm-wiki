# One-Person Business with Claude

## Summary
A one-person AI service business can be structured as a folder-based operating system: Claude Code, reusable skills, client folders, templates, proof assets, and delivery SOPs. The model starts with websites, expands into automations, and compounds into full AI systems when each repeated build becomes a reusable skill.

## Key Takeaways
- The core asset is not just code; it is the skills folder, SOPs, templates, client context, and repeatable delivery processes.
- Start at Level 1 with AI-built websites for proven buyers with bad sites, fresh businesses, or businesses wasting ad spend on weak pages.
- Level 2 adds recurring automations such as follow-up, reminders, review collection, onboarding, invoicing, and dead-lead reactivation.
- Level 3 bundles multiple automations behind a dashboard so the client sees executions, hours saved, money saved, and system health.
- Acquisition runs through two machines: proof-first personal brand/lead magnets and cold email that offers tangible artifacts instead of vague “AI solutions.”
- Sales should diagnose like a doctor: ask pain questions, show a demo, state one price, stay silent, and close around the value the client already quantified.
- Delivery should be plan-heavy: build plan, implementation plan, incremental build, testing/refinement, and second-model verification for complex work.
- After any successful build, skill-ify the process into a generic, standalone capability that interviews the user and can be reused for future clients.
- Pricing should follow a 5x value rule: charge against measurable payroll savings, new gross profit, retained support value, or usage tiers that protect downside.
