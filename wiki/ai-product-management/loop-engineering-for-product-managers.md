# Loop Engineering for Product Managers

## Summary
For PMs, loop engineering is about repeatedly improving product artifacts: research summaries, PRD reviews, signal reports, launch checklists, eval rubrics, and decision support. The PM owns judgment; the loop improves evidence and artifacts.

## Key Takeaways
- PM loops should start with artifacts that already repeat weekly.
- A PM loop needs trigger, action, proof, memory, and stop condition.
- Proof can be eval score, rubric pass, cited evidence, stakeholder-ready draft, or checklist completion.
- Product signal loops are a practical first loop: collect signals, synthesize, compare against strategy, and surface decisions.
- Repositories and versioned documents become product memory.
- PM loops should support decisions, not silently automate judgment.
- Taste must be made inspectable through examples, rubrics, and rejection criteria.
